Biography

Download the PDF version of my resume here. I try to keep my resume as current as possible, but my LinkedIn is always up to date.

I have got a quite strong interest in computer programming since I was very young. It was in the third year of elementary school when I first had access to computer. Finding me quite curious about computer using, my father subscribed to Computer Weekly for me from the beginning of my junior high school. It was at that time that I got hooked on computer, particularly fascinated by programming. While other children spending their leisure time with playing computer games, I was always involved in programming language and the installation of software and hardware.

The first time I programmed by myself practically originated from one issue of this newspaper, which introduced how to make a simple Flash player. The easy step-by-step completion was far from my content and I kept improving the player according to my own thoughts for nearly three years. Finally a multifunction Flash player that I named FlashExplorer became available and continuously updated on the Internet. Since this application, I tied an indissoluble bond with programming.

My major was Microelectronics when I was in college, Hefei University of Techonology in China. I studied couses about computer architecture, computer system organization, database systems and object-oriented programming languages systematically during those four years. As an excellent student of my major, I got the opportunity of becoming an exchange student in I-Shou University in Taiwan for one semester. It was really a good opportunity to broaden my horizon and acquaint myself with the local culture in Taiwan.

After graduated from Hefei University of Technology, I was admitted by University of Southern California Viterbi Engineering School, where I graduated with honor and Electrical Engineering Master of Science Degree. My two years of graduate student life still can't do without programming. I have done several important research projects on computer as the core member of the team, not only accumulating solid professional knowledge, but also developing excellent leadership skills, self-learning ability, sense of teamwork, and innovation spirit. And I studied Analysis of Algorithm, which was the most interesting and exiciting class in my opinion among all the classes I took in USC. I benifited greatly from this couse. The two years has seen my programming research ability and comprehensive quality greatly enhanced.
